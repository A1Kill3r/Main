This website will allow you to encode and decode using several different ciphers. A cipher is a device used to encrypt a string of text which is more difficult for someone else to understand unless they have the key. Some of the ciphers available to use on this website include the well known Caesar cipher and Enigma cipher. The first cipher ever invented was most likely by the Greeks in rougly 400BC and was used to prevent opposing military commanders from reading their messages. Nowadays, there are many different types of ciphers which range from substitution ciphers - which replace the letters of the alphabet with other characters (e.g. 'a' could become '@') - to transposition ciphers - which rearrange the text into a different form (e.g. 'abcd' could be written in a 2x2 block and read column by column).
